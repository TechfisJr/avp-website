# 01 - Creative Direction

## Title of the Experience

**"FROM RAW WOOD. THROUGH NINE UNITS. TO FINISHED PELLETS."**

## Concept

The entire site is a single, unbroken camera move. The visitor never
"navigates" - they travel through a continuous factory process. Scroll is the
only input, and it drives a cinematic dolly through the AVP Wood Pellet line:

```text
1. Raw material storage Unit
2. Woods chipping Unit
3. Wet grinding Unit
4. Buffer storage
5. Drying Unit
6. Recovery Unit
7. Dried grinding Unit
8. Pelletizer Unit
9. Finished product Unit
```

Torrefaction, value upgrading and Black Wood Pellet may appear only as an
optional downstream branch after Unit 9. They are not part of the required core
production flow.

There are no page transitions, no carousels, no generic cards. The 3D world is
the page; typography floats above it like film titles.

## Emotional Arc

| Act | Stations | Feeling | Light |
|-----|----------|---------|-------|
| I - Intake | Raw material storage, chipping | grounded, practical, physical | controlled amber yard/factory light |
| II - Preprocessing | Wet grinding, buffer, drying, recovery, dried grinding | precision, continuity, material control | warm industrial light with dust and moisture cues |
| III - Finishing | Pelletizer, finished product | force, completion, product proof | warm machinery light resolving into clean product contrast |
| IV - Optional extension | Upgrade path after Unit 9 | separate advanced-product logic | premium amber/ember only after finished product |

## Visual Language

- **Darkness as canvas.** Near-black charcoal (#0B0A08) everywhere. Objects are
  revealed by practical light, not placed on backgrounds. Fog does the
  compositing.
- **One continuous factory.** The chipping machine sits partly outside a large
  hall; material then turns left and continues into the same plant for later
  units. Avoid disconnected backgrounds.
- **Physical equipment is the connective tissue.** Use visible logistics and
  machinery: loaded log truck -> raw material storage -> chipper -> conveyors
  -> grinders/buffers/dryer/recovery -> pelletizer -> finished product.
- **No top-down asset views.** All production assets should be framed from side
  or three-quarter side at machine/operator height.

## Palette

| Token | Hex | Use |
|-------|-----|-----|
| `ink` | #0B0A08 | world background / fog base |
| `bone` | #EDE6DA | primary type |
| `amber` | #E8A33D | brand accent, practical machinery light |
| `ember` | #E85D26 | heat and high-energy machine moments |
| `bark` | #8C5A2B | raw wood |
| `moss` | #7BA05B | restrained natural/source accents |
| `pine` | #26382B | deep shadow |
| `frost` | #7FB4C7 | controlled contrast in later production scenes |

## Typography

- **Display:** Fraunces (serif, optical size, soft wonk) - film-title
  headlines.
- **Text/UI:** Space Grotesk - eyebrows, data points, HUD, progress rail.
- Headlines reveal line-by-line with clip masks, tracking-in from 0.02em.
- Eyebrows are letterspaced uppercase 11px with an amber tick mark.

## Sound of the Copy

Short. Declarative. Physical verbs. The copy should explain the current unit
without pretending optional upgrades are part of the base line. Max one short
paragraph per station; the scene carries the information.

## What This Is Not

No stock-photo hero banners. No feature grids. No generic fade-ins. No generic
forest fly-through as the main opening scene. No disconnected machine rooms.
No torrefaction, thermal upgrading or black pellet claim inside the core nine
units. The required message resolves as: raw wood enters, the nine-unit line
processes it, and finished wood pellets come out.
