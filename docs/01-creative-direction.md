# 01 — Creative Direction

## Title of the experience
**“ONE PELLET — From Forest to Flame, and Back.”**

## Concept
The entire site is a single, unbroken camera move. One hero wood pellet is the
protagonist. The visitor never “navigates” — they *travel*. Scroll is the only
input, and it drives a cinematic dolly through fourteen stations of the biomass
value chain, from a night forest to an industrial furnace and back into the
carbon cycle.

There are no page transitions, no carousels, no cards. The 3D world is the
page; typography floats above it like film titles.

## Emotional arc
| Act | Stations | Feeling | Light |
|-----|----------|---------|-------|
| I — Origin | Forest, Collection | reverence, quiet | moonlit green-gold, volumetric fog |
| II — Transformation | Screening → Pelletizing | precision, force | amber sodium-vapor industrial |
| III — Product | Cooling → Warehouse | calm, order | cool teal + clean white |
| IV — World | Logistics, Energy | scale, power | deep blue night + furnace orange |
| V — Return | Circular Economy | hope, closure | dawn green, returning to Act I |

## Visual language
- **Darkness as canvas.** Near-black charcoal (#0B0A08) everywhere. Objects are
  revealed by light, not placed on backgrounds. Fog does the compositing.
- **One material family.** Wood tones (raw bark → fiber → compressed pellet),
  dark brushed steel for machinery, emissive amber for energy.
- **Particles are the connective tissue.** Every transition between stations is
  carried by particles: spores → chips → sawdust → steam → pellets → sparks →
  leaves. Matter is never destroyed, only transformed — that IS the story.
- **The pellet is always on screen** (or its matter is, mid-transformation).

| `ink` | #0B0A08 | world background / fog base |
| `bone` | #EDE6DA | primary type |

| `bark` | #8C5A2B | raw wood |

| `frost` | #7FB4C7 | cooling / QC / logistics |

## Typography
- **Display:** Fraunces (serif, optical size, soft wonk) — film-title headlines.
- **Text/UI:** Space Grotesk — eyebrows, data points, HUD, progress rail.
- Headlines reveal line-by-line with clip masks, tracking-in from 0.02em.
- Eyebrows are letterspaced uppercase 11px with an amber tick mark.

## Sound of the copy
Short. Declarative. Physical verbs. “The forest gives. The mill shapes.
The world burns cleaner.” Max one short paragraph per station; the scene
carries the information.

## What this is not
No stock-photo hero banners. No feature grids. No generic fade-ins. No
“sustainability” greenwash imagery — the circular story is *shown* by the
particle cycle returning to the forest.
